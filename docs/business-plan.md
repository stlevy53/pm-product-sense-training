# The Prototyping PM — Business Plan

**Problem statement:** A new interview round is spreading at frontier companies — AI product sense, where candidates build a working prototype live. Non-technical PMs are unprepared for it, incumbents (Exponent, IGotAnOffer) haven't built prep for it, and the broader skill it tests — defensible trade-off judgment — is the top documented failure mode in PM interviews. Nobody sells training for either.

**Recommendation:** Build a faceless, product-led business selling digital products that teach PMs to prototype with AI tools and defend trade-offs — anchored on the AI product sense interview as the urgent, high-intent entry point. Target: $1–2K/mo within ~6 months on 10–20 hrs/week. No video, no face, no audience-building treadmill.

**Thesis:** The artifact is the argument. A prototype tells 1,000,000 words. Frameworks get you to Hire; defensible trade-offs get you to Strong Hire.

---

## The Customer

Primary: non-technical PMs (3–10 yrs experience) with an active job search and an AI product sense or prototype round coming. Urgent, dated problem; high willingness to pay (stakes are a $150K+ job).

Secondary: working PMs who want to ship prototypes instead of decks to win internal alignment. Same products, evergreen demand.

## Why This Wins

- **Empty search term.** "AI product sense interview" has near-zero real content. First definitive resource ranks and stays ranked.
- **Intent-driven, not personality-driven.** Interview prep is found by searching, not following. No competition with Lenny-scale voices required.
- **The product is the proof.** Working prototypes, prompts, and repos included — incumbents publish think pieces; this ships artifacts. A 20-year non-technical PM who ships tools is the credibility, stated once, in text.
- **Demand tailwind.** AI-fluency demand in PM postings grew ~7x in two years (McKinsey via HBR).

## Unit Economics to Goal

$1–2K/mo ≈ 12 prep packages + 15 toolkits per month, or ~40 toolkit-equivalent sales. At typical Gumroad conversion (1–3%), that's ~1,500–3,000 monthly visitors from search + community + free-tool traffic. Modest, achievable numbers — this does not require an audience.

## 90-Day Roadmap (10–20 hrs/week)

### Phase 1 — Foundation + free wedge (Weeks 1–3)
- Name, Gumroad store, simple landing page, email capture (ConvertKit free tier).
- Build the free Trade-off Drill tool with Claude Code. It's the marketing, the email magnet, and proof of method in one artifact.
- Write article #1: the definitive "AI Product Sense Interview" guide. Long, thorough, the piece that ranks.
- Post the free tool: r/ProductManagement, PM Discords/Slacks, Product Hunt.

### Phase 2 — First revenue (Weeks 4–7)
- Build and launch Toolkit ($39). Content is mostly extraction: the prompts and playbook already exist in how you work.
- Article #2: "How a non-technical PM builds a working prototype in 90 minutes" (with the artifact embedded).
- Launch to email list + communities. **Checkpoint: 10 sales in first 30 days or diagnose before building the flagship.**

### Phase 3 — Flagship (Weeks 8–12)
- Build Interview Prep package ($129). Biggest build; the drills and rubrics are the moat.
- Articles #3–4: long-tail interview queries ("product sense prototype round," "PM trade-off interview questions").
- Launch. Toolkit buyers get an upgrade offer — first warm cross-sell.

### Ongoing (Month 4+, target <5 hrs/week)
- 1–2 SEO articles/month, drill refreshes quarterly, community presence opportunistic.
- Decide on subscription tier only if flagship sells.

## Distribution (all faceless)

1. **SEO** — own the empty term before incumbents write about it. Text only.
2. **Free artifacts** — each free tool is shareable marketing that demonstrates the paid method.
3. **Communities** — substantive answers where interview panic already lives (Reddit, PM Slacks). No broadcasting, no persona.
4. **Email** — capture from day one; every launch lands warm.

Explicitly not doing: LinkedIn thought-leadership cadence, video, follower-count goals.

## Risks & Kill Criteria

- **Niche fills fast.** Exponent could ship this round's prep in a quarter. Mitigation: speed — the 90-day plan exists to be first; drills and artifacts are harder to copy than articles.
- **"AI product sense" round stays rare.** Mitigation: the Toolkit and trade-off drills sell to working PMs regardless; the interview angle is the wedge, not the whole business.
- **Kill criteria:** if the Toolkit hasn't hit 10 sales within 30 days of launch *after* fixing obvious funnel problems, stop and re-evaluate the niche before building the flagship. If the flagship can't reach $1K/mo by month 6, pivot the same assets toward the working-PM (non-interview) buyer or the gaming PM niche.
